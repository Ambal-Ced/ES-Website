Impact of Climate Change

Environment

The impact of climate change on the environment includes rising temperatures, more frequent and severe natural disasters, such as hurricanes, droughts, and wildfires, melting glaciers and ice caps, rising sea levels, shifts in ecosystems and habitats, and disruptions to wildlife and plant species. Climate change causes global temperatures to rise. Glaciers are melting resulting to sea levels to rise. There are more extreme weather events like hurricanes and wildfires. Oceans are becoming more acidic. Animal habitats are being destroyed. The loss of biodiversity is accelerating. As a result of climate change, there will be more heatwaves and alterations to weather patterns.

Social

The impact of climate change on society includes threats to human health from heatwaves, vector-borne diseases, and poor air quality, displacement of populations due to sea-level rise and extreme weather events, food and water insecurity, increased conflicts over resources, and economic disruptions. Climate change creates food and water insecurity. It allows infectious diseases to spread more easily. It forces populations to be displaced and migrate. Vulnerable groups are disproportionately affected. It increases morbidity and mortality rates.The impact of climate change on society includes threats to human health from heatwaves, vector-borne diseases, and poor air quality, displacement of populations due to sea-level rise and extreme weather events, food and water insecurity, increased conflicts over resources, and economic disruptions.social inequality: Vulnerable and marginalised populations, such as those in poverty, indigenous peoples, women, and children, are particularly affected by climate change. These populations frequently lack the means and ability to adjust to the effects of climate change, which worsens the existing social inequalities. Natural disasters can cause harm to communities, while warmer weather can cause illnesses in humans.

Economic

The impact of climate change on the economy includes damage to infrastructure and property from extreme weather events, increased costs for healthcare and disaster recovery, disruptions to agriculture and fisheries, loss of livelihoods in sectors affected by climate-related changes, such as tourism and agriculture, and potential financial instability due to climate-related risks.

Broken Structures: Rebuilding buildings, roads, and other infrastructure after a disaster is quite expensive.

More Expensive Insurance: As a result of businesses having to cover all damage, insurance becomes more expensive as disasters occur more frequently.

Declining Agricultural Production: Food shortages occur as a result of climate change as more livestock dies and more plants become withered as extreme heat kills them.

Impact on Fisheries and Aquaculture: climate change impacts the marine ecosystem and fisheries through ocean warming, changes in ocean currents, and acidification. These impacts interfere with fish stocks and marine habitats, affecting tons of people depending on fishing and agriculture.
