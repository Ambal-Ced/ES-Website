Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global weather patterns, including temperature, precipitation, and extreme events, largely caused by human activities like burning fossil fuels and deforestation. It's crucial to address because it threatens ecosystems, water and food supplies, increases the frequency of extreme weather events, and poses risks to human health and infrastructure. Mitigating climate change is essential to safeguarding the planet for future generations. Global warming of 1.1°C above pre-industrial levels has been caused by over a century of burning fossil fuels and unequal, unsustainable energy and land use. This has led to an increase in the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, which have caused dangerous impacts on nature and people worldwide.


Global Warming

A phenomenon of climate change characterized by the increasing average temperature of the earth’s surface. It is the long-term heating of the earth’s surface. A phenomenon that has been observed starting from the pre-industrial period (year 1850 and 1900). This warming trend has been going on for a long time and its pace has only increased ever since. As the human population has increased, so has the volume of fossil fuels burned. Fossil fuels include coal, oil, and natural gas, and burning them causes what is known as the “greenhouse effect” in Earth’s atmosphere.

Greenhouse Gases

By design, greenhouse gases have kept Earth’s climate habitable for humans and millions of other living beings by trapping heat from the sun. But due to human activities that destroy the standards that have been set by nature, those gases are now out of balance and pose a threat to the well-being of those it initially protected. Some of the greenhouse gases are Carbon dioxide (CO2), Methane (CH4), Nitrous oxide (N2O), and Fluorinated gases such as Hydrofluorocarbons, perfluorocarbons, sulfur hexafluoride, and nitrogen trifluoride. Among those gases, the most dangerous and prominent greenhouse gas is carbon dioxide. And currently, it is at the highest atmospheric levels ever recorded. Greenhouse gas levels are so high primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels that releases these gases into the atmosphere. Which then absorbs solar energy and keep heat close to Earth’s surface, rather than letting it escape into space. That trapping of heat is known as the greenhouse effect.


Carbon Footprint

A measure of how much carbon dioxide an individual sends into the atmosphere. Actions such as riding an automobile, burning leaves, waste disposal, using electricity and even eating something emits carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. Each individual's carbon emissions all adds up and becomes a part of the total emissions on earth.
